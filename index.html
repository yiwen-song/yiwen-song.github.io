About Me

I am a Research Scientist at Google. My research focuses on Large Language Models (LLMs), particularly at the intersection of multimodality, generative AI, and agentic systems. I am passionate about advancing how models reason, plan, and interact with complex, real-world environments.

Recently, my work has centered on enabling computer use agents (CUAs) to learn from human demonstration videos, developing scalable multi-agent frameworks (like blackboard systems), and exploring novel ways to improve LLM problem-solving through test-time scaling and step-by-step planning.

Research Interests

  • Computer Use & Agentic Systems: Building frameworks to scale training data for UI-interacting agents and advancing multi-agent architectures (e.g., Blackboard systems).
  • LLM Reasoning & Test-Time Scaling: Improving complex problem-solving via iterative decoding, affective feedback, and post-training planning trajectories.
  • Multimodal & Foundational Models: Scaling generative models across vision and language, evaluating alignment through calibrated autoraters, and pushing the capabilities of frontier models.
